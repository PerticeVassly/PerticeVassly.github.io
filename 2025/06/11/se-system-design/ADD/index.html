What is Architectural drivers

They are a subset of the requirements that shape the architecture

  • functional requirements, quality attribute requirements, Constraints

But other drivers include

  • Type of system is being designed, Design objectives, Concern

These are the inputs to the desgin process

Functional drivers : Typically involve primary functionality

Quality attribute drivers : Measurable characteristics of interest to users and developers

  • performance …

  • can be specified using scenarios

  • prioritzed

Constraints ： Time & Budget；Technical Limitations； Business Rules & Regulations

Type of systems :

  • Greenfield systems in novel domains;

  • Greenfiled systems in mature domains;

  • Brownfield systems

Design Purpose/Objectives :

  • pre-sales : rapid design

  • custom system : time and costs

Architectural Concerns: additional aspects that need to be considered as part of architectural design but are not expressed as traditional requirements

ADD

Step 1：Review Inputs

Primary functionaliy quality attribute scenarios need to be prioritized

Get the drivers right

Step 2: Establish the Iteration Goal by Selecting Drivers

A design round generally takes the form of a series of design iterations,where each iteration focuses on achieving a particular goal

Establish a “right-sized” iteration goal

  • may be to address a single important driver,

  • may be to make decisions to satisfy a set of similar drivers,

  • may be to make decisions to satisfy a set of relateddrivers

Step 3: Choose One or More Elements of the System to Refine

decomposition into finergrained elements (top-downapproach), combination of elements into coarser-grained (bottom-upapproach), improvement of previously identified elements.

Step 4: Choose One or More Design Concepts That Satisfy the Selected Drivers

Step 4.1: ldentify Design Concepts

reference above

Step 4.2: Selection of Design Concepts

Basic Selection Method

  • Create a pros-cons table listing advantages and disadvantagesof eachalternative design concept to support selection based on drivers

In - depth Analysis Approaches

  • CBAM: Quantitative method using utility - response curves, cost - benefitanalysis, and ROI to select architectural strategies.

  • SWOT: Evaluate strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, andthreatsofalternatives.

Prototype - Based Selection：When analysis fails, create throwaway prototypes

Step 5: Instantiate Architectural Elements, Allocate Responsibilities, and Define Interfaces

Step 6: Sketch Views and Record Design Decisions

The “blueprint” is refined. This may be done inparallel with step 5. Note: This is not full blown documentation but rather sketches.

As you instantiate design concepts you will typicall create sketches. These are initial documentation for your architecture.

Step 7: Perform Analysis of Current Design and Review Iteration Goal and Achievement of Design Purpose

Decisions made at this point are analyzed along with the advances in the overall design process in order to decide if more iterations are necessary.

Design Process Termination Criteria

The design process continues across several iterations

until design decisions have been made for all of the driving architectural requirements

until the most important technical risks haven been mitigated

until the time allotted for architectural design has been exhausted

until the architecture desitn is consumed
